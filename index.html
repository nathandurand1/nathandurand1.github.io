3D-Printed Basketball Shoe

Capstone project: collaborated with Nike and Adidas engineers to design and 3D print a basketball shoe, prioritizing safety and efficiency. Used Fusion 360 to design the complex non-geometric shape and all of its fine details. Conducted an in-depth research study on materials suitable for the shoe — evaluating durability, flexibility, and performance to optimize comfort and functionality.

Fusion 360 3D Printing Materials Research Industry Mentorship

Automatic Bowling Device for Kids with Special Needs

Designed and 3D-printed a switch-activated bowling launcher in Autodesk Inventor that enables individuals with motor impairments to bowl independently. Built for and donated to JP Lord School in Omaha, NE. Gathered feedback from users and iterated on the design to improve usability, accessibility, and overall performance.

Autodesk Inventor 3D Printing Accessibility User Research

Earned FAA Part 107 drone pilot license and led drone-based site surveys, gathering aerial imagery and topographic data that improved land data accuracy and turnaround time for development projects.
